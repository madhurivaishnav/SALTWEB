Responsible Officers for APRA

The licensing regime administered by the Australian Prudential Regulatory Authority (APRA) requires superannuation trustees that are companies to have responsible officers.

APRA's ultimate goal in administering the Registrable Superannuation Entity (RSE) licensing regime is to promote high standards of compliance, risk management and governance within the superannuation industry.

The main purpose of the role of the "Responsible Officer" (RO) in the context of the APRA regime is to enable the RSE to demonstrate that its senior people satisfy the "fit and proper" test under the Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993.

APRA ROs

  • are part of the Registrable Superannuation Entity (RSE) licensing regime

  • are defined in s 10 Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993

  • all staff who fulfil the definition, such as directors, secretary or executives, will be deemed ROs

Being an APRA RO does not impose extra legal duties upon an individual to the body corporate that is the trustee of the superannuation fundor an entity. As APRA ROs are already likely to be "officers" under the Corporations Act 2001, they have the same legal duties to the corporate trustee as if they were not ROs under superannuation legislation.

Before granting an RSE licence, APRA requires the licensee to:

  • meet the "Fit and Proper" standard under superannuation legislation

  • disclose material outsourcing of agreements

  • demonstrate that it will comply with relevant laws and the conditions on the licence

  • have adequate human, technological and financial resources

  • have a risk management strategy

  • have a risk management plan

Meeting the Fit and Proper standard involves APRA examining the individual's:

  • character

  • competence

  • diligence

  • experience

  • honesty

  • integrity

  • judgement

  • educational or technical qualifications, knowledge and skills

  • criminal history and bankruptcy status (if any)

The prime responsibility for ensuring that the RSE licensee's ROs are fit and proper, and remain fit and proper on an on-going basis, lies with the RSE licensee. ASIC or the Federal Court of Australia may disqualify a person from being a RO if they have contravened certain laws.

Under the Corporations Act 2001, "officers" must comply with the following Corporations Act requirements:

  • duty to take reasonable care and diligence s 180

  • duty to act in good faith in the best interests of the corporation and for a proper purpose s 181

  • duty not to misuse position s 182

  • duty not to misuse information s 183

There are a range of civil and criminal provisions in the Corporations Act for a breach of these duties.

Generally, ROs that are directors of the corporate trustee have a right of indemnity from the superannuation fund in respect of a liability incurred while acting as a director of the trustee.

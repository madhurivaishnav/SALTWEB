Environmental obligations under the RMA

Restrictions on land use

New or existing land uses on any particular site will require resource consents if the relevant district plan requires this. Subdivisions will almost always require consent from the local council (with the exception of minor boundary adjustments).

Water uses

The RMA provides that no person is able to take, use, dam or divert any water unless permitted by a regional plan or a resource consent. It is an offence to, for example, take water from a bore or divert a stream on your property without the necessary consents from the council.

Discharges of contaminants

The RMA generally prohibits the discharge of contaminants into or on to land, water or air, unless permitted under the regional plan, regulations, a resource consent, or by existing use rights. ‘Contaminant’ is broadly defined to include any substance, energy or heat which is likely to change the condition of that land or water. It includes gases, odorous compounds, liquids, solids or micro-organisms.

Noise

Occupiers of land have a duty under section16 of the RMA to avoid unreasonable noise, and are required to adopt the ‘best practicable option’ to prevent noise exceeding a reasonable level.

Other environmental effects

A general duty exists for persons to ‘avoid remedy or mitigate’ adverse effects on the environment arising from any activities they carry out. Enforcement action can be taken by the council if adverse effects are “noxious, dangerous or offensive”.

Introduction to Environmental Law

What is the RMA?

The RMA is the main piece of environmental legislation in New Zealand.

What is the purpose of the RMA?

The purpose of the RMA is to promote the sustainable management of resources and control adverse (harmful) environmental effects.

Who is covered by the RMA?

The RMA applies to any ‘person’ wishing to use/develop any natural or physical resource to which the RMA relates. This includes all individuals, companies, organisations, local authorities (regional/district councils) and the Crown.

What happens if I breach the RMA?

If you undertake an activity which is not permitted by a rule in a local authority’s plan, or by a resource consent, or by an existing use right, then you may be liable under the RMA. Infringement notices (instant fines), abatement notices (requiring a person to cease an activity or take remedial action), enforcement orders (a Court order) or prosecutions, are all possible consequences that may be sought against a person found to be breaching the RMA.

What are the maximum penalties under the RMA?

For serious breaches up to two years in prison or a fine of $300,000 for an individual or $600,000 for companies. A further fine may be imposed if commercial gain has resulted from the breach.
